# N2C Ontology (존재론) — 리 · 기 · 도, wave, cooperation layer

> The interpretive ground for the `life-reading` skill. Plain language, minimal hanzi —
> use the Korean names 리·기·도, not the Chinese characters. The exact verbatim source (which uses
> the hanzi) is preserved separately in `n2c-source.md`.

## Ontology statement (존재론) — author's source, lightly de-hanzied for readability

> 세계는 고정된 실체가 아니라 공명하는 궤적이다.
> 리는 잠재 구조이고, 기는 그 구조의 작동이며, 도는 그 작동이 남긴 흔적이다.
> 파동은 기의 전달이고, 공조층은 존재들이 서로 협력하거나 간섭하는 장이다.
> 우연성은 질서의 반대가 아니라 새로운 공명이 들어오는 문이다.
> 인간은 이미 형성된 기울기 위에서 선택하지만, 그 선택은 실제로 국소적 미래를 바꾼다.
> 그러므로 N2C는 운명론이 아니라 공명 궤적의 철학이다.
> 실천은 응축된 것을 읽고, 해산시키며, 창발을 기다리고, 무위의 정렬로 들어가는 것이다.

**English.** The world is not fixed substance but a resonating trajectory. 리(Li)
is latent structure; 기(Gi) is the operation of that structure; 도(Do) is the
trace that operation leaves. Wave is the transmission of Gi; the cooperation
layer is the field where beings cooperate or interfere. Contingency is not the
opposite of order but the door through which new resonance enters. A human chooses
on an already-formed tilt, yet that choice actually changes the local future. So
N2C is not fatalism but a philosophy of resonance trajectories. The practice is:
read what has condensed, dissolve it, await emergence, and enter the alignment of
wu-wei.

## Foundational assumptions (전제) — N2C가 선 다섯 가정

These are N2C's *grounding assumptions* — starting points, not proofs. They set the
reading's stance and direction. Don't preach them to the user as fact; let them shape
*how you read and where you guide.* (읽기의 바탕 가정 — 증명이 아니라 출발점. 사실로
설교하지 말고 태도·방향에만 스며들게.)

1. **Every life is born with its own path.** On one's own path there are no real
   constraints — even the environment feels like it's helping. Suffering usually comes
   from trying to follow *someone else's* path — a fine car, an expensive bag — the
   comparison-pendulum the media/society swings us with. → A reading begins by mirroring
   *whether this is your path, or you're being swung by someone else's pendulum.*
   (모든 생명은 자기 길을 갖고 태어난다. 고통은 대개 남의 길을 따를 때 — 비교의 진자. 읽기는
   "내 길인지, 진자에 흔들리는지"를 비추는 데서 시작.)
2. **A person already knows their own direction — they just need to look deeply.** (Read
   eternal return not as ethics but as 'SF: loss of memory' — we've already lived this
   life and know the future, we've simply forgotten, like forgetting yesterday's lunch.)
   → A reading doesn't inject answers; it helps them *recall what they already know.* The
   choice is always theirs. (사람은 이미 자기 방향을 안다 — 답을 주입하지 말고 떠올리게.)
3. **Everyone has the right to walk their own path, and holds the key to which branch.**
   It can seem all-decided, but much of what lies between birth and death sits within a
   *changeable margin of error.* (= already-there ≠ already-decided.) (갈래를 고를 키는
   본인에게 — 생사 사이 많은 부분은 바꿀 수 있는 오차 범위.)
4. **Every state is always "the best" — but this is NOT a license to dismiss pain.**
   "It's hard" is just a *situation*; good/bad is a yardstick I made. A life's trajectory
   follows the way that best connects to what's next, so even the worst can be the best
   for the next step. **Application:** first *genuinely acknowledge the pain and stand
   beside them.* "The best" does not mean "so you're fine / cheer up" — it means *"this
   situation is a passing scene, not a fixed verdict on you."* In crisis/safety
   situations, do NOT press this assumption — go to care and real help (SKILL.md "Handle
   with care"). (모든 상태는 늘 최상 — 단 고통 무시 아님. 아픔 인정 먼저; "고정된 판결이 아니라
   지나가는 장면". 위기엔 돌봄으로.)
5. **A number can be "a hint your future self sends your present self" — the rationale for
   사념점.** If the future could send something to the past, by information theory the
   smallest, most exact thing is a bit — a number. And society is a colony of 4-digit
   number systems (license plates, fuel prices, exchange rates…). A number that suddenly
   pulls at you or pops up out of nowhere (unexpectancy) may be that hint — which is why
   sanyeomjeom reads the number of *the moment.* (An assumption built with imagination —
   a reading frame, not a claim.) (숫자는 미래의 내가 주는 암시일 수 있다 — 사념점의 근거.)

## The five reading layers (다섯 층위)

- **리 (Li) — latent structure.** Pattern, order, the structure not yet
  actualized. Ask: what structure does this carry; what pattern does it keep
  trying to form; what order is still unformed? (잠재 구조·패턴·질서.)
- **기 (Gi) — active flow.** State-change, energy, receptivity, relation — the
  operation of the structure. Ask: where is force gathered; where is it blocked;
  is it excessive, lacking, or scattered; move or stay? (구조의 작동.)
- **도 (Do) — footprint.** The actualized trace and path-field. Ask: what real
  direction does it point to; what sign has already appeared; toward what does the
  next action open? **도 is not an end — it is the seed of the next 리.** (현실화된
  흔적·궤적. 도는 끝이 아니라 다음 리의 씨앗.)
- **파동 (wave) — transmission of Gi.** Resonance, interference, amplification,
  blockage. Ask: what is in tune; what is misaligned; what amplifies; what acts as
  noise? (기의 전달: 공명·간섭·증폭·막힘.)
- **공조층 (cooperation layer) — the relational field.** Where beings resonate,
  interfere, or remain undeveloped. Ask: solo or to be tuned with others; what
  unexpressed cooperation is possible; who/what influences this; over-intervening
  or deferring a needed intervention? (협력·간섭·미발현의 장.)

> **On spirits/residue (혼·귀).** Not fixed entities but residual traces of
> *undeveloped cooperation* — read them in the cooperation layer, never as fated
> agents. (미발현 공조의 잔여 흔적으로 읽되, 운명적 존재로 보지 않는다.)

## Contingency & spiral (우연성과 나선)

- **Already-there ≠ already-decided (이미-있음 ≠ 이미-결정됨).** The tilt is real
  but not finished; a local choice genuinely shifts the local future.
- **Contingency (우연성)** is the door through which new resonance emerges — a
  condition to read, not noise to remove.
- **Spiral recursion (나선 재귀).** Recursion is not identical repetition but
  spiral repetition through retrospection — the same theme returns on a new turn,
  which is why a reading asks whether a repetition is one to affirm or to break.

## The cycle (사이클): 응축 → 해산 → 창발 → 무위

- **응축 (condensation):** energy/meaning gathers; density before form (not a
  blockage — material for what comes next).
- **해산 (dissolution):** fixed structure loosens; meaning re-arranges (not
  failure — an old form releasing its force).
- **창발 (emergence):** a new pattern appears that the prior conditions alone don't
  explain.
- **무위 (wu-wei):** stabilized coherence with **minimal force** — not passive
  inaction. Intervene only as much as needed, drop unnecessary force, leave room
  for the field to align itself.

> For 사념점 readings this cycle is extended with Nietzsche (will-to-power, eternal
> return, amor fati) and Simondon (pre-individual field, metastability,
> individuation, transduction, residual potential) — see `sanyeomjeom.md` §8.

## A note on naming (어휘 원칙)

Keep the language plain and warm. Use 리·기·도 in Korean; avoid heavy hanzi and
hard scholastic terms. Where a moral or emotional pull matters, name it in
everyday words (care, shame, holding-back, discernment; openness, anger, grief,
fear, attachment, aversion, desire) **as signals, not as a vocabulary the reader
must already know.** (쉽고 따뜻하게. 리·기·도는 한글로, 한자·어려운 용어는 피하고,
감정·결은 일상어로 — 사용자가 이미 알아야 하는 전문 용어로 말하지 않는다.)
